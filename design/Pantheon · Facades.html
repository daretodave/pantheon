Phase 02 · Facade grammar · Pending approval

A shared architecture.
An endless vocabulary.

Every show is a flat, illustrated facade. The grammar is universal — column, frieze, pediment, ornament — but the motifs belong to the show. The promise stays editorial; the surface stays alive.

§ 01 · The four slots

A pediment, a frieze, columns, an ornament.

A facade fits a 1200 × 800 viewBox at 3:2 — a confident editorial ratio that holds at any breakpoint. Every show ships four primitives that snap into these slots. Mass and proportion stay constant; only the vocabulary changes.

① Pediment
The crown
1200 × 240 · triangular · isoceles
② Frieze
The horizontal band
1200 × 80 · tileable · 15:1
③ Column
The vertical motif
160 × 440 · 3× or 5× across
④ Ornament
The repeating mark
80 × 80 · season pages, cards
§ 02 · Composition rules

Six rules that make a facade.

The grammar isn't a kit of parts — it's a contract. These rules guarantee that a new show, designed by a new contributor, lands inside the Pantheon family on first try.

Rule 01

3 columns at desktop, 5 at hero.

Hero facades on the home and show-home use 5 columns spaced 200px on-center. Cards and inline contexts compress to 3 columns at 360px on-center. Even counts are forbidden — the eye needs a center column.

Rule 02

Pediment apex on the vertical axis.

The pediment apex sits at viewBox x=600, dead center. The triangle is isoceles, never scalene. Apex y is constant at 60. This is the only fixed point in the whole grammar.

Rule 03

The frieze tiles. It never centers.

Friezes are designed as a repeating unit and tiled across the 1200px width. The pattern continues off-canvas — never cropped to fit a centerpiece. If the show needs a centerpiece, it lives in the pediment.

Rule 04

Three colors, no more.

Each show ships exactly three colors: a show-paper (background), a show-ink (linework), and a show-primary (the one ceremonial accent). The Pantheon gold from the chrome never appears inside a facade.

Rule 05

Linework before fill.

Every primitive is drawn at stroke-width 3 in show-ink. Fills are reserved for show-primary, used on no more than 15% of the visible area. A facade reads as an etching first, a poster second.

Rule 06

The sigil is the facade, cropped.

Every show's sigil.svg is the pediment + the center column, cropped to a 320 × 320 square. No new artwork. If the facade can't survive being cropped to a square, the facade is wrong, not the sigil.

§ 03 · Primitives cheat sheet

The four files a new show must ship.

Adding a show to Pantheon means producing exactly these four SVGs, in these viewBoxes, against these palettes. Everything else — facade composition, sigil derivation, card thumbnails, share images — is generated.

file 01
column.svg
viewBox 0 0 160 440
stroke 3px · show-ink
fill show-primary · ≤15%
baseline y = 440
file 02
pediment.svg
viewBox 0 0 1200 240
apex x = 600 · y = 0
base y = 240
slope isoceles only
file 03
frieze.svg
viewBox 0 0 1200 80

file 04
ornament.svg

centered · 8px padding
single-color preferred
used at 16px → 80px
§ 04 · Sigil derivation

From facade to badge in one crop.

The sigil is the show's compressed identity — used on cards, share images, page headers, the favicon. It is never drawn separately. Take the full facade, isolate the pediment and the center column, fit to a 320 × 320 square. Done.

survivor.sigil.svg — pediment + center column, fit to 320 × 320. Renders cleanly at any size from 16px (favicon) to a Times Square jumbotron, in theory. at 80×80 · 320×320 — same file
Color rationale

Paper · #0E2A2A reads as ocean-at-dusk: the moment after sunset on a beach in a place very far from home. It is the show's emotional setting, not its lighting.

Ink · #EFE2BD is the warm cream of weathered driftwood, of beach-pebble bone — never bright white. It is what gets exposed when the ocean retreats.

Primary · #D55E36 is the single coral flame of the torch. The brief said no licensed imagery; this is the universal language of fire, not a logo.
